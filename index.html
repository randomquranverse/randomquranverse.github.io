What is the Quran?

The Quran is the holy book of Islam, revealed by Allah (God) to the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) over a period of 23 years. It is considered the final and most complete revelation for humanity, guiding Muslims in every aspect of life. The Quran is written in Arabic and consists of 114 chapters called Surahs, which vary in length. Each Surah is made up of verses known as Ayahs. The Quran contains a total of 6,236 verses (Ayahs), offering wisdom on faith, law, guidance, morality, and personal conduct. Muslims believe the Quran is the literal word of God, preserved in its original form without any changes or alterations since it was revealed. The Quran serves not only as a source of divine knowledge but also as a means to connect with Allah through recitation and reflection. It’s also recited during prayers and special occasions by Muslims around the world. The Quran has a profound impact on the lives of Muslims, shaping their worldview, beliefs, and practices.
